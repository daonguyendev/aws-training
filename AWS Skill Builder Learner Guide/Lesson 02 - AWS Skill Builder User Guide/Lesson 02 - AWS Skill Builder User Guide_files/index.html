AWS Skill Builder User Guide

Learning objectives

In this course, you will do the following: 

  • Learn how to access and navigate the learner dashboard.
  • Understand how to navigate the course catalog.
  • Demonstrate where to find and enroll in learning plans.
  • Know which content is offered as a part of a subscription.
  • Learn helpful tips for using Skill Builder.

Learner dashboard

First, you will explore the learner dashboard. To watch a demonstration on accessing and navigating the learner dashboard, choose the play button. A transcript follows the video.

To access a transcript of the video, choose the following transcript. 

Welcome to this demonstration on using the learner dashboard in Skill Builder. In this demonstration, you will explore the different features of the learner dashboard. 


The Learner dashboard is your home base.


On this page, you can view your courses under My courses. If you don’t see what you are looking for, you can choose View All to see a listing of all available courses. 


From the Learner dashboard, you can navigate to the Classroom Training portal and to your AWS Certification account. You can also navigate to our Ramp-Up Guides, which are sample curricula organized to help you prepare for a cloud role or to dive deep into a particular domain.


Thank you for your participation.

Course catalog

Now, you can start exploring individual courses available in AWS Skill Builder. To watch a demonstration on how to navigate the course catalog, choose the play button. A transcript follows the video.

To access a transcript of the video, choose the following transcript.

Welcome to this demonstration on using the course catalog in Skill Builder. In this demonstration, you will explore the course catalog to find individual courses available in AWS Skill Builder.


To navigate to the course catalog, choose the vertical ellipsis icon in the top left corner.


 This accesses the navigation pane. Now, choose Course catalog.


You can scroll through the entire catalog. To narrow your options, choose Filters.


With the filters panel, you can narrow your results. 


For example, use the Domain filter to find subject matter like compute, database, and machine learning.


You can also filter by your language preference. 


You can filter by training category. Category options include digital courses, self-paced labs, game-based learning, and more. You will learn more about these categories in a moment. 


If you want to see the Learning Plans, filter for them under Type.


And of course, you can search our catalog with keywords. Skill Builder looks for exact matches to your input, so we recommend using simple search parameters before using phrases.


With your options narrowed, you can quickly scan the results and find which courses interest you most.


Skill Builder offers digital courses that you can take at your own pace.


Subscribers can take self-paced labs. These labs are practical exercises using the AWS Management Console that you can experience in an environment without the risk of disruptions or unanticipated expense. 


AWS Jam Journeys are open-ended challenges available to subscribers that test your skills and help you identify knowledge gaps across common AWS use cases.


With Cloud Quest and Industry Quest, subscribers can apply their cloud skills in a game-based learning environment. Players use AWS services to build solutions to solve technical problems within a virtual city. With each completed assignment, players earn rewards to visually transform the landscape and customize their avatar. You can play Cloud Quest: Cloud Practitioner without a subscription. 


Exam Prep gives you confidence on test day. We have courses and question sets, and subscribers have access to official practice exams.


When you find something that interests you, and you want to take a deeper look, choose it. 


This section contains the course description and more details. If you decide to take this course, you can choose enroll and save it for later or begin it immediately.


When you enroll in a course, a course player will open. Make sure your pop-up blocker recognizes Skill Builder as a trusted site.


After you complete the course, you can leave feedback in the Course Feedback Survey.


Thank you for your participation.

Learning plans

Next, you will dive into learning plans. Learning plans are a compiled list of courses, labs, and other trainings that AWS Training and Certification designed to build your knowledge in a specific domain or job role. To watch a demonstration on how to explore learning plans, choose the play button. A transcript follows the video. 

To access a transcript of the video, choose the following transcript. 

Welcome to this demonstration on learning plans. In this demonstration, you will learn how to search learning plans in Skill Builder.


The quickest way to navigate to learning plans is from the course catalog. For Filters, expand the type option and choose Learning Plan. We recommend choosing your preferred language as well. Do NOT filter by domain.


Skill Builder provides dozens of learning plans. These are course collections that help learners achieve a learning goal within a particular domain or for a particular role. 


Some of our learning plans include an assessment that, if passed, will award the learner with a knowledge badge. 


These collections are called Knowledge Badge Readiness Paths. They include a knowledge assessment for the information provided in the learning plan. If you pass the assessment, you earn a digital credential that validates your skills and knowledge for specific AWS Cloud topics. If the learner doesn’t pass, the assessment tells the learners which materials from the learning plan they should review.


There is no charge for earning and sharing a badge. Search knowledge badge to show these paths.


When you select a learning plan, you will see a description of the plan.


To see the list of courses the learning plan contains, choose the Learning Plan Structure tab. If you choose to enroll in this learning plan, Skill Builder will automatically enroll you in every course in that learning plan. The next time you visit the dashboard, you will see several courses to which you have been recently enrolled.


After you enroll in a learning plan, it provides a dashboard that tracks your progress.


Thank you for your participation.

Subscription content

Although AWS Skill Builder offers a plethora of trainings at no charge, some content requires a paid subscription. Subscriptions can be purchased by individual learners or by teams. 

To learn more about which learning content is available at no charge and what is available with a subscription, choose each of the following two tabs.

Most Skill Builder content is available at no charge, including courses, exam prep, practice questions, and Cloud Quest: Cloud Practitioner. Skill Builder provides the following without a subscription:

  • More than 600 on-demand courses and dozens of Learning Plans
  • Standard Exam Prep courses
  • AWS Certification official question sets
  • AWS Cloud Quest: Cloud Practitioner
  • AWS Card Clash

However, a substantial amount of practical learning experiences are only for subscribers, including Builder Labs, Cloud Quest, or Industry Quest. With these experiences, you use the AWS Management Console and AWS Command Line Interface to practice real-life cloud tasks. 


A Skill Builder subscription includes the no-charge learning content, in addition to the following:

  • Unlimited access to more than 125 AWS Builder Labs
  • Enhanced Exam Prep courses with practice materials
  • AWS Cloud Question: Intermediate and advanced roles
  • AWS Industry Quest (Financial Services, HealthCare, Auto & Manufacturing) 
  • AWS Certification Official Practice exams
  • AWS Jam Journey
  • AWS Escape Room: Exam Prep for AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner (CLF-C02)

Helpful tips

As you begin your AWS Skill Builder journey, here are some helpful tips!

To learn more helpful tips for using Skill Builder, expand each of the following four categories.

If you need to unenroll from an individual course, you can do so by choosing the Unenroll from the Course icon in the top-right corner. Note: If a you are enrolled in a course through a learning plan, you cannot unenroll. You can submit a case to AWS Support(opens in a new tab). With this form, you can request that they unenroll you from the learning plan.

Don’t want to unenroll, but want to clean up your My courses view? You can hide a course from the Learner dashboard or the My courses list by choosing the menu icon next to the course and selecting Hide.

You can access the hidden courses from My Courses. In Filters, expand Hidden items and choose Show hidden items only. You can also choose Also show hidden items to view the hidden courses with the rest of your courses.

From the User Menu in the top left corner, you can access My Account. On the Profile section, you can add an avatar, update your name, and provide some high-level details on your role. If you navigate to Preferences, you can change your user interface language, date format, and time zone. If you navigate to Purchases and Subscriptions, you can follow links to log into your AWS Console account and manage your service charges and/or AWS Skill Builder subscription.

Need help? You can access the frequently asked questions, or FAQs, and common issues by navigating to Support from the main Learner dashboard. FAQs cover topics like browser requirements, sign-in options, and other helpful information.
